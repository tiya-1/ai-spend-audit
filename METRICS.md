# METRICS

## North Star Metric

The North Star metric for this product is:

**Completed AI audits per week that generate a recommendation with positive savings.**

This metric matters because the product only creates value if users complete the audit flow and receive actionable recommendations. A visitor landing on the site without finishing the audit does not provide product validation or lead-generation value.

---

## Input Metrics

### 1. Landing Page → Audit Start Conversion

This measures how effectively the homepage communicates the value proposition. If users are not starting audits, the messaging or positioning is weak.

### 2. Audit Completion Rate

This measures whether the audit flow is simple and trustworthy. A low completion rate would indicate friction in the form experience or lack of perceived value.

### 3. Report Request Submission Rate

This measures lead intent. Users who submit emails for reports are significantly more valuable than anonymous visitors.

---

## What I Would Instrument First

The first analytics events I would instrument are:

* Landing page visits
* Audit started
* Audit completed
* Recommendation generated
* Email/report request submitted
* Share link copied

These events would help identify where users drop off in the funnel.

---

## Pivot Threshold

If fewer than 10% of completed audits generate meaningful savings recommendations after the first 500 users, I would reconsider the product positioning.

That would suggest either:

* pricing optimization is not painful enough,
* recommendations are too weak,
* or the market prefers broader AI management tools instead of optimization-focused audits.

At that point I would explore expanding into AI subscription management, usage tracking, or benchmarking features instead of only savings recommendations.
