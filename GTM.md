# GTM

The exact target user for this MVP is a technical founder or engineering manager at a startup with 3–25 employees that actively uses multiple AI tools across development, writing, research, and support workflows.

These users are usually early adopters of tools like Cursor, Claude, GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT Team, and Gemini. Because AI tooling is adopted quickly inside startups, subscription spending often becomes fragmented. Different employees experiment with different tools, duplicate subscriptions appear, and enterprise plans are sometimes purchased before they are actually necessary.

Right before wanting this product, the user is likely:

* reviewing SaaS expenses,
* checking company card transactions,
* discussing budget cuts,
* or searching for alternatives to expensive AI subscriptions.

Typical searches could include:

* “Cursor vs Copilot pricing”
* “best AI coding assistant for teams”
* “ChatGPT Team worth it”
* “Claude vs ChatGPT cost”
* “AI SaaS stack for startups”

These users spend time in:

* r/SideProject
* r/SaaS
* r/Entrepreneur
* Indie Hackers
* AI engineering Discord communities
* Product Hunt discussions
* Twitter/X startup circles
* developer Slack communities

To get the first 100 users with no paid budget, I would focus on direct founder outreach and distribution through communities already discussing AI tooling costs.

The first strategy would be posting real audit breakdowns publicly:

* “This startup was paying for 3 overlapping AI tools.”
* “Potential savings found: $220/month.”

The second strategy would be personalized outreach to indie hackers and startup builders already publicly discussing AI tooling.

The third strategy would be creating comparison-style content:

* “Claude Team vs ChatGPT Team”
* “When Cursor Pro is enough”
* “5 AI subscriptions startups forget they’re paying for”

The unfair distribution advantage is speed and positioning. Most AI tools focus on adding new features, while very few focus on reducing AI SaaS waste. Credex could position itself as the financial optimization layer for AI adoption.

Week-1 traction success would look like:

* 300 landing page visitors
* 80 completed audits
* 15 report requests
* 3 consultation conversations
* at least 1 founder sharing the tool publicly
