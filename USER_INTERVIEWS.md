## Interview 1 — Tulsi, Student Developer Using Gemini Student Access

I spoke with Tulsi, a student developer currently using Gemini Advanced through a free student offer for coding and project assistance.

### Direct Quotes

* “I mainly use Gemini for coding help and debugging.”
* “I’m using the free student access right now, but I probably won’t continue once it becomes paid.”
* “Most students I know keep switching tools depending on what’s free.”
* “I’d use an audit tool if it compared which subscriptions are actually worth paying for.”

### Most Surprising Insight

The most interesting insight was that users often optimize around temporary free credits or educational offers instead of long-term product loyalty. Subscription decisions were strongly tied to affordability rather than platform preference.

### What It Changed About My Design

This conversation influenced how I think about future recommendation logic. It highlighted that many users optimize around free credits and educational offers, so future versions of the product could include recommendations around when paid plans are actually worth upgrading to.

## Interview 2 — Anshika, Student Developer, Side Projects Stage

I spoke with Anshika, a student developer who frequently uses free AI tools for coding assignments and side projects but does not currently pay for subscriptions.

### Direct Quotes

* “I mostly switch between free ChatGPT and Gemini depending on limits.”
* “I don’t really pay for AI tools because the free versions are enough for my work right now.”
* “I usually just wait for limits to reset instead of paying.”
* “If a tool clearly showed when upgrading is actually worth it, I’d probably use it.”

### Most Surprising Insight

The most surprising thing was that even non-paying users still experience confusion around AI pricing and overlapping features. The issue was not only cost, but understanding which plan actually provides enough value.

### What It Changed About My Design

This conversation reinforced the importance of keeping recommendations simple and understandable for smaller users and students, not only teams or enterprises. It also highlighted that future versions of the product could focus more on helping users understand whether premium AI subscriptions are genuinely necessary for their workflow.
